Meaning of nutrition

Nutrition is the process of nourishing the body. For a healthy body, food must have the right nutrients. To sustain health and vitality, nutrition involves eating and drinking the proper amount of food and liquid. Choosing a range of healthful foods is vital since each type supplies various nutrients. Balanced diets, which include foods from all dietary groups, provide nutrients.

 WHO provides the real meaning of nutrition, Nutrition studies in what way nutrients in addition to supplementary components in diet disturb a creature's upkeep, progress, replica, disease, and health. It comprises eating, absorption, assimilation, biosynthesis, catabolism, and excretion. Food availability, palatability, and digestibility determine an organism's diet. Nutrients:

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are essential for proper body function. Carbohydrates are found in foods such as bread, rice, pasta, cereals, and fruits. Carbohydrates are broken down into glucose, which is used for energy.

Proteins

Proteins are another main type of nutrient. They are necessary for the growth and repair of tissues. Proteins are found in eggs, dairy products, fish, meat, and poutry products. The body breaks down proteins into amino acids, which are used for cell growth and repair.

Fats

Fats are another main type of nutrient. They are essential for proper body function. Fats are found in oils, butter, margarine, and fatty fish. The body breaks down fats into fatty acids, which are used for energy and cell growth

Vitamins

Vitamins are another main type of nutrient. They are essential for proper body function. Vitamins are found in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. The body needs vitamins for cell growth and repair.

Minerals

Minerals are another main type of nutrient. They are essential for proper body function. Minerals are found in fruits, vegetables, meat, and dairy products. The body needs minerals for cell growth and repair.

Water

Water is another main type of nutrient. It is essential for proper body function. Water is found in fruits, vegetables, meat, and dairy products. The body needs water for cell growth and repair.

symptoms of poor nutrition

 There are many symptoms of poor nutrition. Some of the most common symptoms include weight loss, fatigue, and weakness. Poor nutrition can also lead to problems with digestion, skin, and hair. If you are not getting the proper nutrients, you may also be at risk for diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and cancer.
